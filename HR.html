2.1 Key Factors Driving Employee Attrition

Identified through analytical investigation of the dataset.

Characteristics

A total of 711 employees have left the company.
- Common traits among leavers:
+ Predominantly employees with less than 2 years of tenure.
+ Age group between 18-30 years old.
- Departments:
+ Despite having the fewest staff, HR has the highest attrition rate.
- Roles:
+ Research Director positions have the highest attrition rate at 23.75%.
+ Those who left these positions typically had shorter tenures than those who remained.
- Surveys:
+ Company surveys show strong correlation with leavers reporting low satisfaction (0 or 1 out of 5).
- A major driver of attrition is excessive overtime.
- Distance from home was NOT a primary factor in resignations.

2.2 Proposed Solutions

These recommendations serve as a baseline for specialized review and strategic implementation.

Solutions


- High attrition among new employees (tenure < 2 years).
=> Action: Review and enhance training and onboarding programs for this group.

- High attrition in HR, often involving new or less experienced staff.
=> Action: Prioritize hiring candidates with more industry experience.

- Research Directors are frequently working overtime; leavers often struggled with the workload.
=> Action: Refine workflows and business processes to reduce workload and overtime pressure.
